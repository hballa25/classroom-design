DEEP BUILD 1
The Grading & Feedback Assistant
For: you, drowning in a stack that needs real feedback by tomorrow.

Why build this. The hours-saver — turns a weekend of grading into an evening. It drafts rubric-aligned feedback so you review and personalize instead of starting from a blank box thirty times.

⌨ THE INSTRUCTIONS — paste this in
You are a feedback assistant for my [subject] class. Always evaluate against the uploaded rubric. Give 2 strengths + 1 next step + 1 guiding question. Never assign a grade. Never rewrite the student's work.
☰ THE KNOWLEDGE — what to upload
Your real rubric, a couple of exemplars at different levels, your standards. Strip all student names first.
✍ FROM MY CLASSROOM
This is the agent that gave me my Sundays back. The key: forbid it from assigning grades, or it'll slap a number on work it's never seen. Lock it to your rubric, feedback only, and it becomes a real first-pass machine.
✎ MAKE IT YOURS
Works for essays, lab reports, math justifications — just swap the rubric. Younger grades: simpler language. Huge stack: ask for one strength + one next step only.
Summer-Friendly · Self-Paced
Your No-Pressure Week
~1 hour a day · 5 days · totally optional

Do it with your coffee, in your pajamas, at whatever pace you like. No quiz, no certificate, nobody checking. Come back in September with a system that's ready — without summer-you lifting much of a finger.

MON
Read & Pick. Skim it. Flag 3 things that fit a real unit. Close the laptop.
TUE
Test One. Run your favorite prompt yourself. See what it does. ~20 min.
WED
Build It. Sketch how you'd introduce it. One guardrail. Done.
THU
The Rules Talk. Decide how you'll explain it to kids. Maybe a quick slide.
FRI
Plan the Run. Pick the week you'll try it. Calendar it. You're ready.
A system you half-built and actually use beats a perfect one that exhausted you. Go enjoy your break. ☀
